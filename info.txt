About io

"io is the global Socket.IO server instance. If an individual socket represents a single, live connection to one specific user, then io is the central control tower or switchboard that manages all active connections simultaneously."

The Key Responsibilities (What it does)
You can explain that you use io for three main architectural patterns in your app:

Connection Management: It listens for incoming connection events (io.on('connection')) and handles authentication handshakes (like verifying JWTs) before letting users connect.

Broadcasting: It has the authority to talk to everyone at once. For example, broadcasting a user's online status to the entire system.

Room-Based Routing: It allows you to group connections dynamically. You can use it to target specific rooms—like io.to('admin-hr').emit()—to send real-time leave notifications to HR staff without needing to know their specific socket IDs
